## HOOK (15 seconds)

My father has never typed a word in his own language. Not because he can't speak it — he's spoken Qashqai his entire life. Because no computer has ever understood it. Today I'm going to show you one that does.

## PROBLEM (20 seconds)

Qashqai is a Turkic language spoken by one and a half million nomadic people in Iran. It has no keyboard standard. No spell checker. No Wikipedia article written in it. And zero representation in any AI training dataset on earth. UNESCO lists it as vulnerable. Linguists estimate we lose a language every two weeks. The speakers who carry Qashqai are aging, and they will not wait for the next funding cycle.

## SOLUTION DEMO (40 seconds)

QashqAI Voice is a platform I built entirely with Claude. Watch what happens when I type a Qashqai phrase.

*[Types: سلام، سن نئجه‌سین؟]*

Three agents fire in sequence. First, a language detector identifies this as Qashqai — not Persian, not Turkish — using Unicode script analysis. No ML model required. Second, a Cultural Guardian validates the content for cultural safety. This gate is mandatory. It runs before Claude ever sees the message. Third, Claude Opus responds in Qashqai Turkic with cultural awareness.

Now I switch to English: "What is the Qashqai word for water?"

Claude returns *su* — سۇ — and explains that for a nomadic people, water determined every migration route, every seasonal camp, every survival decision. The language carries the knowledge.

## ARCHITECTURE CREDIBILITY (20 seconds)

This is not a wrapper around an API. The consent framework is enforced by shell hooks that block the entire data pipeline if speaker permission is unconfirmed. The Cultural Guardian is hardcoded before the reasoning agent — it cannot be bypassed. And the architecture is modular: swap the detection rules and the system prompts, and this platform works for Zazaki, Luri, Balochi, or any endangered language on earth.

## IMPACT VISION (15 seconds)

We have a living dictionary with fifty words across eleven categories. A classroom with phrase cards in five languages. An academic presentation accepted at LDA Berlin. And a roadmap to record the first voice dataset — starting with my father, a native speaker from the Amaleh tribe.

## CALL TO ACTION (10 seconds)

His language has never been inside a computer. I'm here to change that. And I built every piece of it with Claude.
