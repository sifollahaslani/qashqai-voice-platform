## HOOK (15 seconds)

My father has spoken Qashqai his entire life, but he has never typed a single word in his own language. No computer has ever understood it. Today I'm going to show you one that does.

## PROBLEM (20 seconds)

Qashqai is a Turkic language spoken by one and a half million nomadic people in Iran. No keyboard standard. No spell checker. No Wikipedia. Zero presence in any AI training corpus on earth. UNESCO classifies it as vulnerable under the Atlas of Endangered Languages. We are inside UNESCO's Decade of Indigenous Languages — 2022 to 2032 — and the elder speakers who carry this living heritage will not wait for the next funding cycle.

## SOLUTION DEMO (40 seconds)

QashqAI Voice is a consent-first AI platform I built entirely with Claude. Three agents fire in sequence.

*[Types: سلام، سن نئجه‌سین؟]*

First — a Language Detector identifies this as Qashqai, not Persian, not Turkish, using heuristic Unicode analysis. No ML training data required. Second — a Cultural Guardian enforces community consent rules and protects sacred knowledge. This gate is mandatory. It runs before Claude ever sees the message. Third — Claude Opus responds in Qashqai Turkic with full cultural context.

Now I switch to English: "What is the Qashqai word for water?"

Claude returns *su* — سۇ — and explains that for a nomadic people, water determined every migration route, every seasonal camp, every survival decision. The language carries the knowledge.

## ARCHITECTURE CREDIBILITY (20 seconds)

This is not a wrapper around an API. Shell hooks block the entire data pipeline if speaker consent is unconfirmed. The Cultural Guardian is hardcoded before the reasoning agent — it cannot be bypassed. And the architecture is modular: swap detection rules and system prompts, and this platform works for Zazaki, Luri, Balochi — any endangered language on earth.

## IMPACT VISION (15 seconds)

A living dictionary — fifty words, eleven categories, five languages. A classroom with phrase cards and speech synthesis. An academic presentation accepted at LDA 2026 Berlin. Alignment with five UNESCO frameworks including the ICH Convention. And a roadmap to record the first voice dataset — starting with my father, a native speaker from the Darreh-Shuri subdivision.

## CALL TO ACTION (10 seconds)

His language is UNESCO Living Heritage that has never been inside a computer. I'm here to change that — and I built every piece of it with Claude.
