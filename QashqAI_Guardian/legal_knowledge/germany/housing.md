# Housing (Wohnung / Kündigung / Räumung) — Germany

> General information and signposting only. Not legal advice. Verify everything
> case-specific against your own documents and a qualified advisor.

## In short (VERIFIED, general)
- A termination letter (Kündigung) is **not** the same as an eviction. In
  Germany a landlord generally cannot remove a tenant without a **court order**.
- Tenants have strong statutory protections; many terminations can be contested.
- Specialised, low-cost help exists through tenant associations.

## Check on your document (VERIFY)
- The exact **date** of the letter and any stated **deadline (Frist)**.
- Whether it is an *ordinary* or *extraordinary* termination, and the reason.
- Any Rechtsbehelfsbelehrung (how/when to respond). Deadlines are strict.

## Competent institutions (types)
- **Mieterverein / Mieterschutzbund** — tenant association (specialised help).
- **Sozialamt / Jobcenter** — if you cannot pay rent or face homelessness.
- **Schuldnerberatung** — if rent arrears / debt are involved.
- **Amtsgericht** — only relevant once a court case (Räumungsklage) is running.

## Evidence to collect
- The full letter (all pages, with date), the Mietvertrag, proof of payments,
  and all written contact with the landlord.
