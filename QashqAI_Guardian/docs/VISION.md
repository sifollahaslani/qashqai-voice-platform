# Vision

## The problem

For people who are low-income, newly arrived, disabled, or digitally excluded,
the decisive obstacle in a crisis is usually **not a lack of information**. It
is **not knowing the next concrete step** — which office, which deadline, which
document, which human being can actually help. By the time the step becomes
clear, a strict deadline (Frist) has often already passed, and some of those
deadlines are irreversible.

## What QashqAI Guardian is

A multilingual **navigator and signpost**, not an advisor. It triages a
situation, explains the general landscape, names the competent institution
*type*, and hands the person off to qualified human help — in their language.

## What it is deliberately NOT

- It is **not** a lawyer and gives **no** individual legal advice.
- It does **not** predict outcomes or tell anyone what they are "entitled" to.
- It does **not** auto-generate case-specific legal documents. Templates are
  generic and completed by the user.

## Why this framing

A system that issues individualised legal conclusions would, in Germany, likely
constitute a regulated legal service (Rechtsdienstleistung) — and, worse, it
would put the people least able to verify the output at the greatest risk if it
were wrong. Guardian inverts that risk: it keeps the irreversible decisions in
human, qualified hands, and uses technology only for the reversible, low-stakes
work of orientation and translation.

## The QashqAI advantage

Most German legal-aid and orientation resources exist only in German and
English. Guardian's real, defensible edge is the **underserved-language layer**:
Persian, Turkish, Arabic, and Qashqai — pointing people to *real human helpers*
they could not otherwise find.

## Mission

> No human should lose the chance to defend themselves simply because they did
> not know what their next step was.
